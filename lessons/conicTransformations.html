Transformation of Conics

Translation
$T_{(r,s)}$: add 'r' to x values & 's' to y values

Rotation
For a rotation of θ about the origin: $\table x=x\cosθ+y\sinθ; y=-x\sinθ+y\cosθ$
ex) Find an equation of a 60° rotation about the origin:
$\table {x^2}/4+{y^2}/1=1; x^2+4y^2=4; (x\cos60°+y\sin60°)^2+4(-x\sin60°+y\cos60°)^2=4; (0.5x+0.866y)^2+4(-0.866x+0.5y)^2=4; (0.25x^2+0.866xy+0.75y^2)+4(0.75x^2-0.866xy+0.25y^2)=4; 3.25x^2-2.598xy+1.75y^2=4$

Use Discriminant to Identify Conics
$Ax^2+Bxy+Cy^2+Dx+Ey+F=0$
ex) Identify the graph of the equation: $9x^2+4xy+6y^2=20$
Find the discriminant: $\table B^2-4AC; (4)^2-4(9)(6); 16-216; -200$
Since $B^2-4AC<0\text" & "(A≠C)$, the graph of the equation is an ellipse

Angle of Rotation About the Origin
For the general equation $Ax^2+Bxy+Cy^2+Dx+Ey+F=0$
$\table A=C→θ=45°; A≠C\text" and " 0<2θ<360°→\tan2θ=B/{A-C}$
Earlier, we identified the graph of $9x^2+4xy+6y^2=20$ to be an ellipse. Now find θ.
$\table \tan2θ=B/{A-C}; \tan2θ=4/{9-6}; \tan2θ=4/3; 2θ=\tan^{-1}(4/3); 2θ≈53.13°; θ≈26.57°$
