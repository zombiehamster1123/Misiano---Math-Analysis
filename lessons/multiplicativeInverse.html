Multiplicative Inverse

  • What you multiply a matrix by to get the identity back
  • Exists for some square matrices
  • $AA^{-1}=A^{-1}=I_n$
Inverse of a $2^{nd}$ Order Matrix
If the determinant ≠ 0(nonsingular), the inverse exists

If $A=[\table a, b; c, d]$ & $|\table a, b; c, d|≠0$, then $A^{-1}={1}/{|\table a, b; c, d|}[\table d, -b; -c, a]$
Ex) Find the multiplicative inverse of $A=[\table -1, 4; 3, 2]$
$\table A^{-1}, =, 1/{|\table 1, b; c, d|}[\table d, -b; -c, a]; , =, 1/{|\table -1, 4; 3, 2|}[\table 2, -4; -3, -1]; , =, 1/{(-2-12)}[\table 2, -4; -3, -1]; , =, -1/14[\table 2, -4; -3, -1]; , =, [\table -2/14, 4/14; 3/14, 1/14]$
