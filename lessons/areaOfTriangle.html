Area of Triangle

The Law of Sines & Cosines can be used to find values needed for the area formula

ex) Find the area of $∆ABC\text" if "a=18.6,A=19°20'\text" & "B=63°50'$. Round your answer the nearest tenth.

$\table \html"<b>1) use law of sines to find b or c</b>";{18.6}/{\sin19°20'}=b/{\sin63°50'};b\sin19°20'=18.6\sin63°50'; b={18.6\sin63°50'}/{\sin19°20'}; b=50.4$ $\table \html"<b>2) find </b>" \bo ∠ \bo C; ∠C=180-∠A-∠B; ∠C=180-19°20'-63°50'; ∠C=96°50'$ $\table \html"<b>3) solve for the area of ∆ABC</b>"; K=1/2ab\sinC; K=1/2(18.6)(50.4)\sin96°50'; K≈465.4\text" "units^2$

If 3 side lengths are given (SSS), use Hero's Formula to find the area
$\table K=√{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}; \text"where "s=1/2(a+b+c)$

ex) Find the area of $∆ABC \text" if "a=20", b=30" \text" & "c=40"$. Round answer to the nearest tenth
$\table s=1/2(a+b+c); s=1/2(20+30+40); s=1/2(90); s=45$ $\table K=√{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}; K=√{45(45-20)(45-30)(45-40)}; K=√{45(25)(15)(5)}; K=√{84,375}; K≈290.5in^2$

Area of a Circular Segment
Circular Sector - entire wedged shaped area
Circular Segment - shaded portion of the circular sector
$S=1/2r^2(θ-\sinθ)$, where θ= central ∠ in radians
ex) Find the area of the circular segment to the nearest tenth with a central angle of 150° and a radius of 11.5".
$\table \text"1) convert degrees to radians"; θ=150(π/{180})={5π}/6$ $\table \text"2) find the area"; S=1/2r^s(θ-sinθ); S=1/2(11.5)^2({5π}/6-\sin{5π}/6); S≈140.1in^2$
