Logarithmic Functions

Exp Function Log Function
$(base)a^{(exp)x}=(log)y$ $log_{(base)a}(log)y=(exp)x$
$2^5=32$ $log_{2}32=5$

Exp & Log Functions are Inverses
You switch the x & y to get the inverse of a function, so its graph is a reflection over the line $y=x$

Properties of Logarithms
Product Property $log_{b}mn=log_{b}m+log_{b}n$
Quotient Property $log_{b}m/n=log_{b}m-log_{b}n$
Power Property $log_{b}m^p=plog_{b}m$
Property of Equality $log_{b}m=log_{b}n→m=n$
