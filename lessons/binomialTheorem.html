Binomial Theorem
let $n∈Z^{+}$, then...
$(x+y)^n=x^n+nx^{n-1}y+{n(n-1)}/{1•2}x^{n-2}y^2+{n(n-1)(n-2)}/{1•2•3}x^{n-3}y^3+...+y^n$

ex) use the binomial theorem to expand... $(m+3)^4$
$\table (m+3)^4, =, m^4+4m^{4-1}(3)+{4(4-1)}/{1•2}m^{4-2}(3)^2+{4(4-1)(4-2)}/{1•2•3}m^{4-3}(3)^3+(3)^4; , =, m^4+12m^3+{12•9}/{2}m^2+{24•27}/{6}m+81; , =, m^4+12m^3+54m^2+108m+81$

Equivalent Form of the Binomial Theorem
This form is useful when finding individual terms of an expansion: $(x+y)^n=∑↙{r=1}↖n{{n!}/{r!(n-r)!}x^{n-r}y^r$
ex) Find the $4^{th}$ term of: $(2x-3y)^6$ (n=4, so r=3)
$\table (2x-3y)^6, =, ∑↙{r=1}↖6{{6!}/{r!(6-r)!}(2x)^{6-r}(-3y)^r}; , =, {6!}/{3!(6-3)!}(2x)^{6-3}(-3y)^3; , =, 720/36(8x^3)(-27y^3); , =, -4320x^3y^3$
