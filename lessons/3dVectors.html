Vectors in 3D Space

Video

Written as an ordered triple, ${AB}↖{→}$ is $(x_2-x_1, y_2-y_1, z_2-z_1)$

The magnitude of ${AB}↖{→}$ is $|{AB}↖{→}|={x^2+y^2+z^2}$

Given $a↖{→}=(x_1,y_1,z_1)$, $b↖{→}=(x_2,y_2,z_2)$ & k is any real #...
Vector Operations in 3D Space
Addition $a↖{→}+b↖{→}=(x_1+x_2, y_1+y_2, z_1+z_2)$
Subtraction $a↖{→}-b↖{→}=(x_1-x_2, y_1-y_2, z_1-z_2)$
Scalar Multiplication ${ka}↖{→}=(kx_1,ky_1,kz_1)$

Given $a↖{→}=(7,5,6)$, $b↖{→}=(4,1,3)$ & $k=3$
Vector Operations in 3D Space
Addition $a↖{→}+b↖{→}=(7+4, 5+1, 6+3)=(11,6,9)$
Subtraction $a↖{→}-b↖{→}=(7-4, 5-1, 6-3)=(3,4,3)$
Scalar Multiplication ${3a}↖{→}=(3•7,3•5,3•6)=(21,15,18)$

Unit Vector - vector with a magnitude (length) of 1 unit
$i↖{→}=(1,0,0)$
$j↖{→}=(0,1,0)$
$k↖{→}=(0,0,1)$

Writing vectors as the sum of unit vectors
Point $(4,9,8)$ becomes $4i↖{→}+9j↖{→}+8k↖{→}$
