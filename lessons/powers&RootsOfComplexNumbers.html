Powers & Roots of Complex Numbers

DeMoivre's Theorem

$[r(cosθ+\i\sinθ)]^n=r^n(\cos(nθ)+\i\sin(nθ))$

ex) Find $(1-√{3}\i)^6$
$\table r=√{x^2+y^2}; r=√{1^2+(-√{3})^2}; r=√{4}=2$
$\table θ=\tan^{-1}(y/x); θ=\tan^{-1}({-√{3}/1}); θ=-π/3=-1.047^r=-60°$
$\table [r(\cosθ +\i\sinθ)]^n=r^n(\cos(nθ)+\i\sin(nθ)); [2(\cos{-π}/3+\i\sin{-π}/3)]^6=2^6(\cos{-6π}/3+\i\sin{-6π}/3); 64(\cos(-2π)+\i\sin(-2π)); =64(1+\i0); =64$

The 'n' Distinct Roots of a Complex #

Radians
$\table (x+y\i)^{(1/n)}=(r[\cos(θ+2πk)+\i\sin(θ+2πk)])^{{1/n}}; =r^{1/n}(\cos{{θ+2πk)}/n+\i\sin{(θ+2πk}/n)$
Degrees
$\table (x+y\i)^{(1/n)}=(r[\cos(θ+360k)+\i\sin(θ+360k)])^{{1/n}}; =r^{1/n}(\cos{{θ+2πk)}/n+\i\sin{(θ+2πk}/n)$

ex) Find three cube roots of -8.
$\table r=√{8^2+0^2}=8; θ\tan^{-1}(0/8)+π=π$
 $\table -8=-8+0\i; (-8+0\i)^{1/3}=(8[\cos(π+2πk)+\i\sin(π+2πk)])^{1/3}; =2(\cos{π+2πk}/3+\i\sin{π+2πk}/3)$
Next, let k=0, 1 & 2 to find the three cube roots...

$\table k=0; =2(\cos{π+2π(0)}/3+\i\sin{π+2π(0)}/3); =2(\cosπ/3+\i\sinπ/3); =2(1/2+\i{√{3}}/2)=1+\i√{3}$ $\table k=1; =2(\cos{π+2π(1)}/3+\i\sin{π+2π(1)}/3); =2(\cosπ+\i\sinπ); =2((-1)+\i(0))=-2$
$\table k=2; =2(\cos{π+2π(2)}/3+\i\sin{π+2π(2)}/3); =2(\cos{5π}/3+\i\sin{5π}/3); =2(1/2+\i({-√{3}/2}))=1-\i√{3}$
the three cube roots $\table -2; 1+\i√{3}; 1-\i√{3}$

Solve Polynomial Equations
Solve $x^3+1=0$

$\table r=√{(-1)^2+0^2}=1; θ=\tan^{-1}(0/{-1})+π=π$ $\table x=(-1)^{1/3}=(-1+0\i)^{1/3}; (-1+0\i)^{1/3}=(1[\cos(π+2πk)+\i\sin(π+2πk)])^{1/3}; =1^{1/3}(\cos{π+2πk}/3+\i\sin{π+2πk}/3); =\cos{π+2πk}/3+\i\sin{π+2πk}/3$
Next, let k = 0, 1 & 2 to find the three roots...

$\table k=0; =\cos{π+2π(0)}/3+\i\sin{π+2π(0)}/3; =\cosπ/3+\i\sinπ/3; =1/2+{√{3}}/2\i$ $\table k=1; =\cos{π+2π(1)}/3+\i\sin{π+2π(1)}/3; =\cosπ+\i\sinπ; =(-1)+\i(0); =-1$
$\table k=2; =\cos{π+2π(2)}/3+\i\sin{π+2π(2)}/3; =\cos{5π}/3+\i\sin{5π}/3; =1/2-{√{3}}/2\i$ the 3 roots of $x^3+1=0$ are: $\table -1; 1/2 ±{√3}/2$
