Simplifying Complex Number

recall... $a+b\bo i$
where a & b are real #'s
& i is the imaginary unit

recall... $\i=√{-1}$
$\i^2=√{-1}•√{-1}=-1$
$\i^3=\i^2•\i=-\i$
$\i^4=\i^2•\i^2=1$

To simplify powers of $\i$, divide exponent by 4 & if...
remainder = 0 $\i^0$ $1$
remainder = 1 $\i^1$ $\i$
remainder =2 $\i^2$ $-1$
remainder = 3 $\i^3$ $-\i$

When adding or subtracting complex #s combine their real parts & their imaginary parts
Addition Example Subtraction Example
$(4+5\i)+(1-3\i)$ $(4+5\i)-(1-3\i)$
$=[4+1]+[5\i+(-3\i)]$ $=[4-1]+[5\i-(-3\i)]$
$=5+2\i$ $=3+8\i$

When multiplying complex #s... use FOIL
Multiplication Example
$\table (4+5\i)(1-3\i); =4-12\i+5\i-15\i^2; =4-7\i-15(-1); =4-7\i+15; =19-7\i$

When dividing complex #s... multiply the numberator & denominator by the conjugate of the denominator
$\table {4+5\i}/{1-3\i}=({4+5\i}/{1-3\i})({1+3\i}/{1+3\i}); ={4+12\i+5\i+15\i^2}/{1+3\i-3\i-9\i^2}; ={4+17\i+15(-1)}/{1-9(-1)}; ={4+17\i-15}/{1+9}; ={-11+17\i}/10; =-11/10+17/{10}\i$
