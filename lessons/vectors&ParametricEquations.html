Vectors & Parametric Equations

Direction Vector - a vector used to describe the slope of a line
Parameter - the independent variable t (time)

Vector Equation of a Line
a line through $P_1(x_1,y_1)$ & parallel to vector $a↖{→}=(a_1,a_2)$ is represented by...
$(x-x_1,y-y_1)=t(a_1,a_2)$ (t = time)

ex) Write a vector equation of a line through $P(1,5)$ a parallel to vector $a↖{→}=(-7,2)$
$\table (x-1,y-5)=t(-7,2); (x-1, y-5)=(-7t, 2t); x-1=-7t, y-5=2t; x=1-7t, y=5+2t$

a line through $P_1$(x_1, y_1) & parallel to vector $a↖{→}=(a_1,a_2)$ is represented by...
$\table x=x_1+ta_1; y=y_1+ta_2$

ex) Write the parametric equations for the line through $P(1,5)$ & parallel to $a↖{→}=(-7,2)$

$\table x=1-7t; x-1=-7t; {x-1}/{-7}=t$ $\table y=5+2t; y-5=2t; {y-5}/2=t$ $\table {x-1}/{-7}={y-5}/2; -7(y-5)=2(x-1); -7y+35=2x-2; -7y=2x-37; \text"Slope-Intercept Form: " y=-2/7x+{37}/7$
