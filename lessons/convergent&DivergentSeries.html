Covergent & Divergent Series

Convergent Series - an infinite series with a sum, or limit

Divergent Series - an infinite series with no sum, or limit
ex) Is this series convergent or divergent: $1/25+1/250+1/2500+1/25000+...$?

$\table \text"Find r..."; r={1/250}/{1/25}=1/10$ Since $|r|<1$, the series has a limit & is convergent

ex) Is this series convergent or divergent: $7+14+21+28+...$?

$\table \text"Find d"; d=14-7; d=7$ Arithmetic series have no limit & is divergent

ex) Is this series convergent or divergent: $1/12+1/3+4/3+16/3+...$?

$\table \text"Find r"; r={1/3}/{1/12}=4$ Since $|r|>1$ the series has no limit & is divergent

Ratio Test for Convergence
used when a series is neither arithmetic nor geometric
$r=\lim↙{n→∞}{{a_{n+1}}/{a_n}}$
If $r=1$, the test provides no information
$a_n$ & $a_{n+1}$ are 2 consecutive terms of a series of positive terms
If the limit exist, the series is: convergent if $r<1$ or divergent if $r>1$
ex) use the ratio test to determine if this series is convergent or divergent: $1+1/{(1•2)}+1/{(1•2•3)}+1/{(1•2•3•4)}+...$?
Find the $n^{th}$ term, then use ratio test...
$\table a_n=1/{1•2•3•...•n}; a_{n+1}=1/{1•2•3•...•(n+1)}$ $\table r=\lim↙{n→∞}{{a_{n+1}}/{a_n}}; r=\lim↙{n→∞}{1/{1•2•3•...•(n+1)}}/{1/{1•2•3•...•n}}; r=\lim↙{n→∞}{1•2•3•...•n}/{1•2•3•...•(n+1)}; r=\lim↙{n→∞}{1/{(n+1)}}=0; \text"Since "r<1\text", the series is convergent"$
