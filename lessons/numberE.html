Compounding Interest
$A=P(1+r/n)^{nt}$
A = total (or ending) amount
P = principal (initial amount)
r = interest rate per time period
n = number of times compounded per year
t = time

ex) How much is a \$3,500 investment worth after $3{1}/2$ years at 6.4% compounded monthly?
$\table A=P(1+r/n)^{nt};A=3500(1+{0.0064}/12)^{12(3.5)}; A=3500(1+{0.0064}/12)^{42}; A≈\$4\text","376.14$ $\table A=\text"total amount"; P=\$3\text","500; r=6.4%=0.064; n=12; t=3.5$

as n (the # of times compunded per year) approaches infinity, the compound interest formula approaches e

th enumber e is known as the "natural exponential" since it occurs naturally in math & the physical sciences, just as π occurs in geometry

Newton's Law of Cooling
$y=ae^{-kt}+c$
y = temperature (in F°) of a cooling object
a & k = constants related to the cooling object
t = time (in minutes)
c = temperature of the medium surrounding the cooling object
