Sigma Notation & the $n^{th}$ Term

$∑↙{n=1}↖{∞}1/{n^2}$
$∞$ - ending index variable
$n=1$ - beginning index variable
$1/{n^2}$ - general term

ex) $\table ∑↙{n=1}↖3 n^2=1^2+2^2+3^2; =1+4+9=14$

$\table ∑↙{n=1}↖{∞} 3({1}/2)^{n+1}, =, 3(1/2)^2+3(1/2)^3+3(1/2)^4+...; , =, 3/4+3/8+3/16+...$
$\table \text"Find r"; r={3/8}/{3/4}=1/2$ $\table \text"Infinite series, so use..."; S_n={a_1}/{1-r}={3/4}/{1-1/2}={3/4}/{1/2}=3/2$ $\table ∴\text" "∑↙{n=1}↖{∞} 3({1}/2)^{n+1}=3/2$
